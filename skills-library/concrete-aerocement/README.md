## Skill Modules

### [xanthan-gum-method.md](xanthan-gum-method.md)
**How to activate soap foam in concrete using xanthan gum stabilization**

- Mix ratios (5 oz xanthan per 80 lbs dry mix)
- Dawn Ultra dish soap procedure (foam activation)
- Common failure modes (dense concrete, foam collapse)
- Expected porosity: 60–80% air, visible pores ≥1mm
- Time: 8 minutes from dry mix to pourable state

### [21-day-wet-cure-protocol.md](21-day-wet-cure-protocol.md)
**Curing schedule for maximum strength**

- Day 1–3: High-frequency misting (every 2 hours)
- Day 4–21: Regular misting (2× per day)
- Temperature management (50–75°F optimal)
- Failure indicators (cracking, mold, premature drying)
- Post-Day-21 dry-down (7–10 days, surface whitening)

### [activated-carbon-pyrolysis.md](activated-carbon-pyrolysis.md)
**Produce activated carbon from waste for aerocement additives**

- Feedstock: charcoal briquettes, scrap wood, waste biomass
- Pyrolysis oven design (drum, rocket stove, solar)
- Activation (steam or chemical)
- Application: 5–10% by volume in aerocement for water absorption
- Cost: $0.50–$2.00 per pound vs. $5/lb purchased

### [ferrocement-tank-construction.md](ferrocement-tank-construction.md)
**Build copper-coil heat storage tanks**

- Tank frame (rebar grid, 1:2:3 cement:sand:gravel)
- Copper coil spiral windup (¾" OD, ~50 feet per tank)
- Insulation integration (1–2 ft foam on exterior)
- Pressure testing (25–50 PSI verification)
- Integration with thermal loop (Tank A hot after panel, Tank B cold after labyrinth)

### [pneumatic-hog-pliers-technique.md](pneumatic-hog-pliers-technique.md)
**Finish aerocement surfaces without damage**

- Tool: Stanley pneumatic nailer or Harbor Freight equivalent
- Technique: Low-pressure tapping (40–60 PSI) to smooth surface
- Avoids: Trowels (drag out pores), wire brushes (too aggressive)
- Result: Sealed surface, full strength, maintains porosity
- Alternative: Sanding with 120-grit + sealer (see wood-finish-analog.md)

## Material Sources

### Portland Cement
- **Home Depot:** 50 lb bags Portland cement ($8–$10)
- **Local landscape supply:** Bulk bags (5–10 lb sacks, $0.50–$1/lb)
- **Recycled:** Crushed concrete as partial replacement (free from demolition sites)

### Xanthan Gum
- **Amazon:** 5 lb bags, ~$30 ($6/lb) — most economical
- **Local co-ops:** Often stocked; ask for bulk pricing
- **Bulk supplier:** Mountain Rose Herbs, Vitacost
- **Food-grade only** — industrial xanthan is identical, cheaper ($2–$3/lb)

### Dish Soap
- **Dawn Ultra:** Dollar General, Walmart, any grocery
- **Why Dawn?** High foaming coefficient, stable bubbles, food-safe
- **Substitutes:** Dr. Bronner's castile (works), homemade lye soap (less predictable)

### Copper Coil & Tubing
- **HVAC supply stores:** ¾" OD soft copper coil, 50–100 ft rolls ($40–$60)
- **Home Depot/Lowes:** Limited stock, higher cost
- **Recycled:** Salvage yards, old AC units (verify purity, pressure-test)

### Foam Insulation
- **Home Depot:** 4" rigid XPS (Styrofoam), R-24, $20–$25 per sheet
- **Never use spray foam** — interacts with wet aerocement, difficult to control
- **DIY alternative:** Cellulose (newspaper fibers) + adhesive (experimental)

## Safety Warnings

⚠️ **Portland Cement** — caustic, can burn skin
- Wear gloves, avoid prolonged skin contact
- Wash immediately if exposure occurs

⚠️ **Pyrolysis (activated carbon)** — high heat, toxic fumes
- Perform outdoors only
- Do not inhale smoke
- Use proper ventilation

⚠️ **Pressure testing (ferrocement tanks)** — explosion hazard
- Do NOT exceed 50 PSI during test
- Use pressure gauge (not guessing)
- Perform in open area, no one nearby
