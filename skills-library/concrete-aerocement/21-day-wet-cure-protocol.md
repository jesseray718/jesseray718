# 21-Day Wet Cure Protocol
## Aerocement Curing Schedule for Maximum Strength

**Duration:** 21 days of active misting + 7–10 days dry-down | **Difficulty:** Easy (monitoring only) | **Cost:** $0 (water only)

---

## What This Achieves

A **slow, continuous hydration** that:
- Maximizes compressive strength (up to 500 PSI at Day 28)
- Prevents cracking (common in fast-cured foam concrete)
- Allows internal porosity to stabilize
- Creates a **durable, lightweight** final product

Skip the wet cure → concrete fails within 1–2 years. This protocol is **non-negotiable**.

---

## Why 21 Days?

**Portland cement hydration** follows a kinetic curve:
- **Day 1–3:** Rapid strength gain (30–50% of final)
- **Day 7:** ~70% of final strength
- **Day 21:** ~90% of final strength
- **Day 28:** ~100% of final strength (reference standard)
- **Day 90+:** Slow continued hydration (pozzolanic reactions)

**Aerocement needs all 21 days** because:
1. Thin foam walls (1–2mm) cure slower than solid concrete
2. Air pockets concentrate hydration heat locally; misting dissipates it
3. Xanthan gum (present in fresh mix) can slow hydration if left dry

## Timeline Overview

| Phase | Days | Misting | Cover | Temperature |
|-------|------|---------|-------|-------------|
| Initial set | 1–3 | Every 2 hours | Yes (cracked) | Keep 65–75°F |
| Early strength | 4–7 | 2× daily | Optional | Keep 65–75°F |
| Mid-strength | 8–14 | 2× daily | No | Keep 50–75°F |
| Final cure | 15–21 | 2× daily | No | Keep 50–75°F |
| Dry-down | 22–28 | Stop | No | Natural airflow |

## Phase 1: Initial Set (Days 1–3)

**Goal:** Prevent rapid evaporation, prevent cracking

### Day 0 (Pour Day)

- [ ] **Cover immediately** after 1–2 hours surface set
- [ ] Use **plastic sheeting** or canvas (not sealed — needs air)
- [ ] Prop cover **¼ inch off surface** for air circulation
- [ ] Cover protects from direct sun & wind (both cause rapid drying)

### Days 1–3: High-Frequency Misting

**Misting schedule:**
- **Every 2 hours**, 24/7 (set phone alarms)
- Morning alarm: 6 a.m. → 8 a.m. → 10 a.m. → 12 p.m. → 2 p.m. → 4 p.m. → 6 p.m. → 8 p.m. → 10 p.m.
- Nighttime: easier if system is automated (sprinkler on 30-min timer)

**Misting technique:**
- Use **garden hose spray** (mist setting, not jet)
- Wet surface **until it glistens** (not dripping wet, not dry)
- Target: moisture visible on surface but not pooling

**Why 2 hours?** Aerocement dries 5–10× faster than normal concrete due to high porosity. At 2-hour intervals, surface never fully dries, preventing shrinkage cracks.

**Temperature monitoring:**
- Ideal: **65–75°F**
- If **>85°F:** Mist more frequently (every 1.5 hours) + add shade cloth
- If **<50°F:** Slow hydration; reduce misting to 3× daily (too much moisture + cold = poor strength)

### Surface Check (Day 1 Evening)

- [ ] Surface should be **firm to touch** (not soupy, not hard)
- [ ] **Hairline cracks**: normal, not a failure
- [ ] **Wide cracks (>1/8"):** too-fast drying, increase misting frequency

### Mold Risk (Days 1–3)

**Indicator:** Black/green spots on surface

**If present:**
- Increase airflow (prop cover higher, add fan)
- Continue misting (mold ≠ failure)
- Mold dies once surface dries (Day 22+)

---

## Phase 2: Early Strength (Days 4–7)

**Goal:** Transition from preventing cracking to building strength

### Remove Cover (Day 4)

- [ ] **Uncover the panel** — aerocement now needs air circulation
- [ ] Keep in shade (direct sun still risks cracks)
- [ ] Misting continues: **2× per day** (morning, evening)

### Misting Schedule (Days 4–7)

- **Morning (8 a.m.):** Mist surface until glistening
- **Evening (6 p.m.):** Mist surface until glistening
- Mist even if it rained; rain ≠ sufficient saturation

### Surface Progression

| Day | Appearance | Strength |
|-----|-----------|----------|
| 4 | Pale gray, tacky surface | ~30% |
| 5 | Gray, surface dries between mistings | ~45% |
| 6 | Light gray, visible pores | ~55% |
| 7 | Pale gray/white in shaded areas | ~70% |

### Compressive Test (Optional, Day 7)

**If you want confidence:**
- Find a small concrete scrap from another project (same mix)
- Tap with hammer; should resist (not crumble)
- If sample crumbles: this mix is weak; check:
  - Was xanthan added to dry mix?
  - Was water amount correct?
  - Has it been misting 2× daily consistently?

---

## Phase 3: Mid-Strength (Days 8–14)

**Goal:** Build final strength while preventing late-stage cracks

### Misting Schedule (Days 8–14)

- **Morning (8 a.m.)** and **evening (6 p.m.)**
- Can reduce frequency to **1× per day** if humidity is naturally high (>60%)
- **Never skip** — even 1 skipped day can cause internal micro-cracks

### Weather Adaptations

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Rain | Still mist afterward (ensures saturation) |
| Dry/hot (>80°F) | Mist extra (3× daily) |
| Humid (>70%) | Can reduce to 1× daily |
| Freeze risk (<32°F) | Stop misting; cover loosely to reduce evaporation |

### Surface Progression

| Day | Appearance | Notes |
|-----|-----------|-------|
| 8–10 | Pale gray/tan | Strength ~75%, OK to handle gently |
| 11–14 | Off-white, chalky | Strength ~85%, ready for light load (not yet full) |

### Cracking Check (Day 10)

- [ ] Inspect surface for **new cracks**
- [ ] **Hairline (<1/32") to thin (1/16")**: normal, not critical
- [ ] **Wide (>1/8") or spreading**: increase misting, check humidity
- [ ] **If cracking continues:** Possible causes:
  - Cement too old (>3 months)
  - Material had impurities
  - Xanthan gum concentration was low
  - Dry period too early (Day 7–14 is critical)

---

## Phase 4: Final Cure (Days 15–21)

**Goal:** Reach full strength, prepare for dry-down

### Misting Schedule (Days 15–21)

- Continue **2× daily** (morning, evening)
- Surface should be **predominantly white/off-white** by Day 18
- **This is the final misting phase** — after Day 21, stop completely

### Day 21 Inspection

**Confirm cure is complete:**
- [ ] Surface is **hard to scratch** with fingernail (should require real effort)
- [ ] **No visible moisture** when surface is pressed with palm (should be dry, not clammy)
- [ ] **Smell:** neutral, slightly alkaline (not sour or fermented)
- [ ] **Color:** pale white or very light gray (not still tan)

### Final Wet Cure Checkpoint

**Before stopping misting:**
- Surface must be **visibly hard** (pass thumbnail test)
- If surface is still **soft/chalky:** Extend misting 3–5 more days
- If surface is **hard but still tan:** Continue misting (color will lighten in dry-down)

---

## Phase 5: Dry-Down (Days 22–28)

**Goal:** Remove internal moisture for thermal performance

### Stop Misting (Day 22)

- [ ] **No more water** — let it dry naturally
- [ ] Aerocement needs **7–10 days** to lose enough moisture for optimal thermal performance
- [ ] **Do not force dry** (heat gun, oven) — causes internal cracking

### Dry-Down Progression

| Day | Surface Color | Moisture Status | Readiness |
|-----|---------------|-----------------|----------|
| 22 | Pale gray | Very moist | Keep drying |
| 24 | Lighter gray | Damp | Continue |
| 26 | Off-white | Slightly damp | Almost ready |
| 28 | Bright white | Dry to touch | Ready for sensors |

### Moisture Check (Day 28)

**Press palm on surface for 10 seconds:**
- [ ] No visible moisture on palm → **ready for sensors**
- [ ] Slight dampness → **wait 2–3 more days**
- [ ] Wet/clammy → **not ready yet** (possible cure issue)

### Why Dry-Down Matters

Residual moisture **decreases thermal efficiency:**
- 1 kg water per 1 m² panel = ~80W thermal loss
- Fully dry aerocement insulates 15–20% better
- Sensor readings need 48+ hours dry time for accuracy

## Temperature & Humidity Management

### Ideal Conditions

| Metric | Optimal | Acceptable | Problematic |
|--------|---------|------------|-------------|
| Temperature | 65–75°F | 50–85°F | <50°F or >95°F |
| Humidity | 60–80% | >50% | <40% |
| Rain | None | OK, mist after | Extended rain → excess saturation |
| Wind | None | Light OK | Strong (>10 mph) → rapid evaporation |

### Extreme Weather Responses

**Hot & Dry (>85°F, <40% humidity)**
- Mist 3× daily instead of 2×
- Add shade cloth if possible (reduces surface temp 10–15°F)
- Never let surface fully dry between mistings

**Cold (<50°F)**
- Reduce misting to 1× daily
- Cover loosely to retain heat
- Cure will be 2–3× slower (OK, just takes longer)
- **Below 32°F:** Stop misting entirely; water expands when frozen, damages structure

**Rainy (extended moisture)**
- Still mist at scheduled times (ensures saturation even if surface looks wet)
- If raining during mist time: skip that misting (rain counts)
- Watch for mold: increase air circulation

## Troubleshooting

### Cracking During Wet Cure

| Symptom | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Hairline cracks (parallel, every 1–2 ft) | Shrinkage during set | Continue misting; normal, will not propagate |
| Wide/spreading cracks (>1/8") | Too-fast early drying | Increase misting; shade panel |
| Random deep cracks | Cement failure or material contamination | Continue cure; document for troubleshooting |

### Surface Soft After Day 21

| Indicator | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Thumbnail scratches easily | Extend misting 5–7 more days; check cement freshness |
| Tacky/gummy feeling | Xanthan concentration too high; not a failure, just slower; wait longer |
| Mold present | Normal; will die once dry-down starts |

### Mold or Algae Growth

- **Appearance:** Black/green spots or film on surface
- **Cause:** High humidity + lack of airflow
- **Response:** Increase airflow (add fan, remove cover barrier) — do NOT reduce misting
- **When removed:** Mold disappears after dry-down (Day 22+); not a permanent defect

### Temperature Swings (Freeze/Thaw)

- **If freezing during wet cure:** 
  - Stop misting immediately (ice expands, cracks concrete)
  - Cover loosely to slow evaporation
  - Resume misting after thaw
  - May extend cure 2–3 days

## Misting Automation (Optional)

If you can't manually mist 2× daily:

### Simple Timer Setup
- **Timer-controlled solenoid valve** on garden hose
- **Set to 15 minutes every 12 hours** (night off, day on; or 24/7)
- **Cost:** $40–$80 for timer + valve
- **Result:** Hands-off misting, consistent moisture

### Sprinkler System
- **Misting nozzles** (not full spray, use "drip irrigation" setting)
- **30-minute intervals** or 2× daily timer
- **Advantage:** Works in high heat, unattended
- **Disadvantage:** Overkill for small panels; use only if you have >3 nodes
