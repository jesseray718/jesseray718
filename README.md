## Active Project: AeroCement Ecosystem

**Volumetric thermal/cryo energy and agritech nodes** -- passive solar systems that produce cold storage, preserved food, and mechanical power with no compressor, no refrigerant, and no grid connection.

Repository: [jesseray718/aerocement](https://github.com/jesseray718/aerocement)

### The Core Idea

An open-cell concrete panel infused with activated carbon captures ~98% of incident solar energy as heat. That heat drives a thermodynamic loop:

1. **Solar panel** heats air volumetrically (air flows *through* the panel, not past it)
2. **Desiccant bed** strips humidity from the air
3. **Underground tunnel** filled with wet porous concrete at ~55 F ground temp
4. **Latent heat exchange** drops outlet air to ~35 F even when it's 100 F outside
5. **Temperature differential** between hot and cold batteries drives Stirling engines and TEG modules

The key metric is **surface area per unit volume (m2/m3)**. Open-cell porosity at 70-85% creates internal exchange surface orders of magnitude beyond flat plates or pipe walls. That's what makes passive cooling at this scale possible.

### What It Powers

- Cold storage and freeze-drying
- Aquaponics and spirulina cultivation
- Beekeeping infrastructure
- Water circulation pumps and fans
- Low-voltage electricity via TEG

### Who It's For

Rural and low-income communities lacking reliable cold chain infrastructure. Built from locally-sourced materials. Replicable without specialized labor. Governed by the people who use it.

## Tokenomics: Proof of Physical Work (PoPW)

Tokens are minted for verified real-world outputs only -- not speculation.

| What earns tokens | Verification |
|---|---|
| Verified node build | Photo + sensor data + community audit |
| Verified uptime | Automated telemetry |
| Kg of freeze-dried food | Logged output + spot checks |
| Code/design/doc contributions | GitHub-linked commits |

Tokens grant access to land, resource credits (cold hours, water, energy), and governance votes. Utility/credit + governance token, not a speculative security.

Legal structure: 501(c)(3) nonprofit + LLC.
