# AeroCement Ecosystem
The first complete OpenRoot node implementation.
Passive solar-thermal cold storage, heating, and mechanical power.
Built from concrete, activated carbon, water, and sunlight.

**Node Zero:** Sikeston, Missouri, USA — building now, June 2026
**Builder:** Jesse McMillen
**DOI:** 10.5281/zenodo.20639511
**License:** CC-BY-SA 4.0 (hardware) | GPL v3 (software)

## The Innovation
Volumetric open-cell aerocement — porous concrete infused
with activated carbon. Air flows THROUGH the material.
Millions of microscopic pores per cubic meter.
Every pore wall is a heat exchange surface.
Orders of magnitude more surface area than any smooth system.

## Hard Constraints
1. 21-day wet cure — non-negotiable
2. Desiccant at fresh air intake — before labyrinth
3. Tunnel filled SOLID with open-cell aerocement — not lined
4. Two separate tanks — Tank A hot, Tank B cold
5. Never claim >100% thermodynamic efficiency
