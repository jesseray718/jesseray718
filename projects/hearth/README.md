# The Hearth
Community-scale energy sovereignty.
Open-source. CC-BY-SA 4.0. No patents.

## Six Systems

1. Solar chimney — black aerocement column, 60-80°C,
   stack effect drives airflow, filters air through carbon
2. Capillary geothermal cooler — wet aerocement block
   buried 10ft, exit air 35-40°F, zero electricity
3. Passive airflow loop — cool air in, warm air out via
   chimney, continuous breeze, zero electricity
4. Earth battery — buried rocks/gravel, stores heat
   for nighttime Stirling operation
5. Stirling engine + flywheel — converts heat to rotation,
   belt-driven appliances, fans, mills, pumps, generators
6. Rocket mass heater — backup wood/algae burner,
   closed carbon loop

## Disaster Relief Shelters
Geodesic domes from cardboard treated with silicone
and diluted acetone. Waterproof. Fire resistant.
Aerocement struts with click-flange assembly.
Cost: ~$45 per small dome. Assembled in hours.

## Delta-V Vehicle
Stirling-powered transport.
Hot side: solar concentrator or biomass burner.
Cold side: ram air + aerocement thermal blocks.
Silent. Zero liquid fuel. Zero emissions.

## Community Hubs
Shelter + workshop + tool library + seed bank + training.
One hub trains 50 builders.
50 builders build 500 homes.
